Essential gardening tools

Tools can be your best friends when growing native plants! Get to know more about the most important ones to make the most of your gardening journey!

Gloves & Boots

The perfect shoes, gloves and hat make gardening easier and more fun. Protect your face from the sun, your hands from the thorns and your feet from the mud.

  • Keeps your hands and feet dry.
  • Protects your hands & feet from thorns and splinters.
  • Select a pair of durable, well-fitting gloves and boots.

Hand Trowel

A trowel is a small hand tool used for digging, applying, smoothing, or moving small amounts of viscous or particulate material.

  • Garden trowels are used for digging small holes, such for planting bulbs, annuals or perennials.
  • Useful for transplanting bedding plants and taking out weeds.
  • Trowel blades should be cleaned, sanitized and wiped dry after each use.

Spade

A spade is a tool primarily for digging, comprising a blade – typically stunted and less curved than that of a shovel – and a long handle.

  • Useful for digging holes, edging, lifting sod, moving small mounds of dirt.
  • A sharpened garden spade is especially good at cutting through the tough matted roots of sod.
  • Its handle-to-blade angle makes it easy to use your body's weight and leg muscles to do the work.

Garden Fork

A garden fork is a gardening implement with a handle and several (usually four to six) short, sturdy tines.

  • Useful for turning soil and to dig into dense soil
  • Useful for moving loose materials such as piled hay, compost, or manure.
  • The tines allow the implement to be pushed more easily into the ground.

Rake

A garden rake is meant for moving soil — you can break up and move clods of dirt and smooth soil with both the tines and the back of the head.

  • Useful for raking up leaves and debris
  • Useful for removing the death plants from lawns
  • Useful for preparing flower beds and cutting weeds

Hoe

A hoe is an ancient and versatile agricultural and horticultural hand tool used to shape soil, remove weeds, clear soil, and harvest root crops.

  • Most kinds of hoes can be used to scrape the surface of the soil and loosen the top inch.
  • Draw hoe can be easily used to cultivate soil to a depth of several inches
  • Scuffle hoe can be used to cut the roots of, remove, and disrupt the growth of weeds.

Watering Can

A watering can is a portable container, usually with a handle and a funnel, used to water plants by hand.

  • The watering can with fine spray can deliver water more gently.
  • Plastic cans can contaminate your water, which is something to regard if you are watering edibles.
  • Watering should be focused on the root zone, as only roots need access to water, not the leaves.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why should I care for my gardening tools? They get dirty when I use them anyway.

    Well-maintained gardening tools will ensure they are in good condition and ready to be used anytime. By doing this, you can not only prevent rust but your tools can work better and last longer too.

  • How often should I clean my gardening tools?

    Ideally, gardening tools should be cleaned after every use. This helps remove soil that can cause cross-contamination to plants, especially if they are used to remove disease plants.

  • Why can’t I leave my tools out in the garden? Its easier for me to use.

    Leaving gardening tools out in the garden is convenient but this exposes the tools to the forces of nature and cause them to become rusty faster. They may not be able perform at their optimal level when you need to use them.

  • Can I still use gardening tools that are rusty?

    It is not advisable to use rusty gardening tools because they can hurt your hands and your plants! Sharp tools inflict less damage than dull ones and require less effort to use.

  • Is it necessary to sharpen my gardening tool?

    If you are using your gardening tools to do a lot of cutting, then you can sharpen them once a year using a whetstone, metal file or other sharpening device. Make sure your tools are completely dry before sharpening them.
