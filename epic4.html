Native Grasses

Native plant species offer many varities that can match your every requirement.

Berry Saltbush

The berries on the Berry Saltbush can be eaten fresh or made into jam if you have the inclination and the time as they are very small. They are a little sweet with a salty note.

  • It's leaves are high in protein and can be grazed by sheep
  • Higher leaf salt levels make it less flammable (Fire retardant)
  • Dense fast growing drought tolerant groundcover.

Chocolate Lily

Simply push the small black seeds into seed sowing mix any time from spring through to late summer and their single leaf will appear within four weeks. When they are large enough to hold with your thumb and forefinger, transplant into pots to grow on for the garden.

  • Small lily that’s perfect for rockeries and small patches in the home garden
  • Edible flower that has a chocolate scent.
  • Their flowers appear from September through to January.

Grass Leaf Trigger

Prefers full sun or semi shade. Grows in damp areas or areas subject to retaining moisture for several months each year.

  • Spikes of small pink or white flowers from September to January.
  • Fruit consists of a spike of capsules each containing very fine red seed.
  • May be short lived.

Thin Leaf Wattle

This plant is more sprawling when growing under trees and forms a compact small bush when growing in the open.

  • Flowers from Aug to Oct.
  • Withstands extended periods of dryness once established.
  • Fast growing with a medium life span (about 20 years).

Yellow Bulbine Lily

A great plant for rockeries, flower gardens and containers. Looks lovely in large clumps. Can be propagated from seed or by division.

  • A great native alternative to daffodils, with the added bonus of fragrance!
  • A long flowering period, from spring to early autumn.
  • It likes to be kept moist, as it will die off in dry periods

Native Wildflowers

Native plant species offer many varities that can match your every requirement.

Billy-Button

Propagate from seed, which germinates in a couple of weeks, normally. Great cut flowers, long lasting, stout stems, with an eye catching shape. Can also be used for drying.

  • Long flowering, from spring to summer.
  • Very frost tolerant, and can take dry periods and coastal sites.
  • Grows from an underground rhizome, so if the plant dies off, it can resprout.

Button Everlasting

This everlasting daisy has long-lasting and showy flowers.It is spectacular in flower; it may be grown in containers or rockeries or used in mass plantings and is an excellent dried flower.

  • Prefers well drained soils.
  • Good butterfly food plant, used by Australian Painted Lady butterfly
  • Insects and ants are known to eat seeds and flowers are pollinated by insects.

Common Rice Flower

Hairy stems. Leaves are opposite and decussate in this Pimelea sp. Develops suckering roots where the branches touch the soil.

  • Widespread, growing in sand, sandy loam or skeletal soil overlying rock.
  • Locally common in heathland and drier woodland.
  • Flowers between the months of September to January.

Tall Bluebell

An attractive, tufted Australian native perennial herb with soft, linear leaves.

  • White flowers appear in spring and summer.
  • Excellent for a massed display in a welldrained soil in full-sun or partshade.
  • Soil should be kept moist but not water-logged.

Tufted Bluebell

This graceful perennial ground cover, native to all Australian mainland states, grows to 40cm high and 30cm wide with suggested planting spacing of 20cm.

  • It prefers well drained soils and full sun.
  • Despite its delicate appearance Wahlenbergia is remarkably tough.
  • It has a lifespan of up to 5 years and can be pruned severely in winter.

Native Climbers

Native plant species offer many varities that can match your every requirement.

Common Appleberry

This native climber makes a fantastic garden and landscape plant as it grows in a wide range of conditions.

  • Tough and adaptable and provides nectar which attracts native birds such as Honeyeaters
  • It will thrive with very little maintenance in good conditions, and can survive in harsh positions such as under Eucalyptus.
  • Clusters of bell-shaped pink and white flowers are produced throughout Spring.

Love Creeper

This creeper, whilst beautiful, can be difficult to establish and grows best in dappled and semi shade.

  • Attracts birds.
  • Low water requirement.
  • Flowers in Spring.

Purple Cora Pea

Plant this charming native climber where its rambling growth can be enjoyed. Excellent for growing among other plants as it doesn't dominate the other plants.

  • Propagate from seed that should be treated with boiling water to soften the seed coat.
  • Also known as the “Happy Wanderer”
  • Could be grown as a ground cover if it is denied access to other plants or objects to clamber over.

Small-leaf Bramble

Rambling, small shrub. Deeply toothed pinnate leaves with 3-5 leaflets. Bright green upper surface on leaf and a silver lower surface.

  • Small clusters of red berries are produced from December-April.
  • Flowers have male and female organs and are pollinated by insects..
  • Flowers from October-December. Raspberry like fruits produced.

Small leaved Clematis

A climbing plant that scrambles through trees and shrubs up to 4 metres with long narrow light green leaves and masses of creamy flowers and seadheads with fluffy white silvery beards.

  • It will smother small plants in a garden bed.
  • It provides nesting habitat for birds and attracts bees.
  • Flowers in Spring.

Native Shrubs & Trees

Native plant species offer many varities that can match your every requirement.

Black She-Oak

Erect dark tree to 8 m with dark deeply-fissured bark. The leaves are actually branchlets. Female flowers reddish to crimson whereas the dark brown male flower spikes are crowded.

  • Found on well-drained soils, sands, clays and rocky slopes, especially near the coast.
  • Male and female flowers are found on separate plants.
  • Flowers between the months of March and June.

Common Health

An attractive medium sized open shrub that is worth growing for the showy masses of bell flowers produced from autumn to spring. Peak flowering is in winter, helping to brighten the garden and feed honey eating birds which love them.

  • Great for floral work, and can range from deep pink to red and white forms.
  • This plant needs a moist but well drained slightly acid soil, and will grow in sun to part shade.
  • Frost hardy. Can be short lived.

Hop Goodenia

Highly ornamental for more open locations, wider verges, median strips, parks and reserves. Planted singly as a colourful background in mixed plantings or en-mass as a barrier or informal hedge.

  • Can become straggly, responds to pruning.
  • Attracts native butterflies and insects, caterpillar food plant.
  • This plant is indigenous to the following botanical regions of South Australia.

Prickly Currant Bush

Prickly, slender, open, upright shrub, 2-4m high. Very small, crowded shiny olive-green leaves.

  • Drought sensitive. Tolerates salt.
  • Protected, semi-shaded position in moist soil.
  • Seedlings usually appear in flush of germination, up to 5 months after sowing.

Showy Bossiaea

It is a hairy-stemmed shrub growing up to 2 m in height and spread. Its triangular, stalkless leaves are alternate, opposite or whorled and 5–20 mm long.

  • Requires full sun, semi-shade.
  • Profuse yellow and red pea flowers August to December.
  • Tolerates coastal exposure and light frosts.
